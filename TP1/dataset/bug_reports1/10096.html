    You know how in 4.xx you can click on and hold down on the back and formward buttons when applicable to bring up a list of up to x url's before or after the one your at? In IE it has the same thing except is does it with a small button beside the forward and back buttons (looks like this > [back][] )
To get the list on IE you click on the small buttons instead of clicking on and holding downon the big buttons.
How about making it so that when this feature is implemented in Mozilla that it does it this way instead of the normal netscape way. It seems easier for newbies this way and wont interfere with the the buttons.
Also being able to configure in prefrences the amount of url's it will allow in the drop down box may be usefull.
(I think it's 15 now, how about giving the user the option to allow anything from 1 to x ? If the amount they
allow is so large that opening it up with that many url's may cause a crash then a pop up warning could allert them
of this prior to okaying it.

    Yes we want to add these drop-downs to back and forward, but unlike MS apps to
the left of the back button and to the right of the forward button to keep the
symmetry between the two, and to keep back and forth in close proximity, since
users are using them often together. (I know this sounds unusual, but looks and
works well, so lets try that). behavior should be like this: on mouseover both
the button and popdown hilite, on mousedown over the popup, only the popup
depresses, on mousedown over the button only the button depresses, on disabled
both the popup and the button are disabled.
I disagree with adding more stuff to the prefs, since those are already
overloaded with many things few people need and are getting harder and harder to
navigate. Lets just settle on 10 items for each back and forward. Re-assigning
to Don for implementation.
