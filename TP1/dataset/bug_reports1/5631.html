    Ladies and/or Gentlemen:
Please accept my apologies for just firing off a bug report in the manner I'm
about to (probably misfiled under webtools) but your bug reporting system is
rather detailed (nice work) and I haven't had the time to familiarize myself
with it.
Anyhow, I've developed a Java based RAID management application for my
company's RAID storage products (MicroNet Technology -- the product is Genesis).
I'm very interested in running this on the Apple platform (mainly G3) as Apple
has chosen to make my company's product their exclusive storage recommendation
for their new OS X server. Your plugin is brilliant and actually runs my app
better than IE using MRJ2.1.1 (for reasons I won't bore you with -- but I'm sure
you're pleased to hear it). The only problem that I've experienced is that modal
dialogs are not modal. The dialog works just fine it is simply not modal. The
app works as expected running under Apple's AppletRunner. I'm part of the MRJ
seed program over at Apple and have access to several of their engineers. They
tell me that enforcement of modality would be the responsibility of the
application (I guess in this case the plugin or Navigator?) and is not enforced
at the OS level (I'm really more a Windows developer and just happen to be doing
alot of Apple stuff on this project, so I don't really have much insight
myself)... So to conclude this rambling, Apple has knocked the ball into your
court on this issue and I was wondering if anyone could respond? Unfortunately I
make use of modal dialog boxes in such a way that this otherwise minor problem
is very significant to me. Besides, I've always been a dedicated Netscape user
since the beginning and I'd love to have all our customers administering their
RAID arrays whilst running the application in Navigator on their Mac!!!! (Is
this a great time or what?)

Thanks for your time and consideration,

Steve Holly
Senior Software Engineer
MicroNet Technology, Inc.
steve@sbholly.com
